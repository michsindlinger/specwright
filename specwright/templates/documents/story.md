## Brand Voice in Storytelling

### Narrative Tone

| Aspect | Our Approach |
|--------|--------------|
| **Perspective** | [FIRST_PERSON_WE / SECOND_PERSON_YOU] |
| **Tense** | [PRESENT / PAST_FOR_STORIES] |
| **Style** | [CONVERSATIONAL / PROFESSIONAL / PLAYFUL] |
| **Length** | [CONCISE / DETAILED] |

### Storytelling Do's

- Start with the customer's world, not the product
- Use specific details (numbers, names, scenarios)
- Show transformation, don't just tell benefits
- Include moments of tension and resolution
- End with a clear next step

### Storytelling Don'ts

- Don't lead with features
- Don't use jargon or buzzwords
- Don't make the brand the hero (customer is the hero)
- Don't be vague about the problem
- Don't forget the emotional journey

## Validation Questions

Before finalizing the story, verify:

- [ ] Does the story start with the customer's pain, not our solution?
- [ ] Is the villain clearly defined (external, internal, philosophical)?
- [ ] Does our product position as the guide, not the hero?
- [ ] Is the transformation specific and measurable?
- [ ] Does the story evoke the right emotions?
- [ ] Is it authentic to our brand and founders?
- [ ] Can team members retell it consistently?
